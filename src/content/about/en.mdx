# My Journey: From 1,248 Ignored Slides to World's Best Storyteller

## The Story That Changed My Story

> The year was 2004. The CEO couldn't get past slide 8. 1,248 slides were waiting. 27 brands would die if he said no.

That was the scene that redefined my career. Faced with an exhausted executive and a R$500 million project about to be rejected, I did the only thing my gut screamed at me to do: I ditched PowerPoint and turned 1,248 slides into theater. The CEO cried. The project was approved. And I discovered my mission: **to prove that no presentation needs to be boring** [1].

My name is Fernando Palacios. I am the grandson of a filmmaker, the son of an engineer and a psychologist. I was supposed to be an engineer, but a question haunted me: **why do some stories change everything while others change nothing?** This obsession led me to explore uncharted territory, which earned me the nickname "the Christopher Columbus of Storytelling" [2].

## My Philosophy: The Science Behind Emotion

> "Just because something is serious, doesn't mean it has to be boring."

I believe that every human is wired for stories. It's our operating system. Yet, 99% of corporate communication ignores this. I exist to fix this problem, one presentation at a time.

### My 3 Pillars:

1.  **Clarity is Power**: If I can't explain it in 30 seconds, I haven't understood it properly.
2.  **Emotion is Logic**: Data convinces, but emotion moves to action.
3.  **Practice Leads to Magic**: Storytelling is not a gift, it's training. It's a muscle that can be developed.
